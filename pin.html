Pin

A pin is a tactic where a piece cannot move without exposing a more valuable piece behind it. Pins restrict movement and create pressure.

Key Concepts

  • Absolute Pin: The pinned piece cannot legally move (e.g., king behind).
  • Relative Pin: Moving the pinned piece loses material.
  • Ideal Pinning Pieces: Bishops, rooks, and queens.

Example Position

A bishop on g5 pins a knight on f6 to the queen on d8 — the knight is stuck!

Pins are great for immobilizing defenders and setting up future attacks. Use them to control key squares and win material.
