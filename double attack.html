Double Attack

A double attack is a tactic where one move creates two simultaneous threats. It’s a broader concept that includes forks and discovered attacks.

Key Concepts

  • Two Threats: The opponent must respond to both.
  • Overload: Often forces a concession or loss of material.
  • Common Types: Forks, checks + captures, threats + pins.

Example Position

A queen moves to h5, threatening checkmate on h7 and attacking a loose bishop on b5 — a double attack!

Double attacks are versatile and can be created by any piece. Look for ways to combine threats in a single move.
