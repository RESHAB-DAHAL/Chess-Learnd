Skewer

A skewer is a tactic where a more valuable piece is attacked and forced to move, exposing a weaker piece behind it. It’s the reverse of a pin.

Key Concepts

  • Linear Attack: Often executed by bishops, rooks, or queens.
  • Forced Movement: The front piece must move, revealing the target.
  • Ideal Targets: King in front, rook or queen behind.

Example Position

A rook on e1 attacks a king on e7. The king moves, and the rook captures the queen on e8 — a perfect skewer!

Skewers are most effective along open files and diagonals. Use them to win major pieces with precision.
