Zwischenzug

Zwischenzug (German for "in-between move") is a surprising intermediate move played before an expected sequence. It interrupts the opponent’s plan and often turns the tables.

Key Concepts

  • Interruptive Tactic: Played in the middle of a forcing line.
  • Surprise Factor: Catches opponents off guard by changing the tempo.
  • Game-Changer: Can shift the evaluation of a position dramatically.

Example Position

Black expects White to capture a rook, but instead white plays Reb8+ giving a check — forcing the king to move — and only then white captures, being at a 2-point advantage from a 3-point disadvantage.

Tips for Using Zwischenzug

  • Look for forcing moves (checks, threats) before automatic recaptures.
  • Use zwischenzugs to disrupt combinations or escape pins.
  • Always ask: “Do I have a better move before continuing the sequence?”

Zwischenzug is a hallmark of advanced tactical awareness. Spotting these moments can elevate your play and surprise even strong opponents.
