Discovered Attack

A discovered attack occurs when moving one piece reveals an attack from another piece behind it. This tactic can be devastating, especially when the revealed attack targets a high-value piece or delivers check.

Key Concepts

  • Hidden Power: The attacking piece is initially blocked by a friendly piece.
  • Trigger Move: The front piece moves, often with its own threat, revealing the attack.
  • Double Trouble: The opponent must respond to both threats, often losing material.

Example Position

Imagine a bishop on b2 and a pawn on c3. When the pawn moves to c4, the bishop suddenly attacks a rook on g7 — a classic discovered attack.

Tips for Using Discovered Attacks

  • Look for aligned pieces (rooks, bishops, queens) behind pawns or minor pieces.
  • Use the front piece to create a secondary threat — forks work well here.
  • Discovered checks are especially powerful, often forcing the king to move.

Train your eye to spot hidden attackers waiting to be unleashed. Discovered attacks can flip the game in a single move.
